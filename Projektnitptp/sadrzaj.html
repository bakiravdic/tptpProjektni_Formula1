F1 Engineering & Technology

Deep dive into the world of carbon fiber, aerodynamics, and hybrid power units.

The Power Unit

Modern F1 cars use a 1.6-liter V6 turbo hybrid engine. It's not just an engine; it's a thermal efficiency masterpiece reaching over 50% efficiency.

Aerodynamics: Ground Effect

The 2026 regulations emphasize ground effect to allow cars to follow each other closely, making overtaking more about driver skill than clean air.

Steering Wheel Controls

DRS

Activates the rear wing flap to reduce aerodynamic drag and increase top speed on straights.

Engine Mode

Controls engine power maps to balance between maximum performance and fuel/energy saving.

Radio

Enables real-time voice communication between the driver and the race engineers in the pit wall.

Differential

Adjusts how torque is distributed between the rear wheels to optimize traction during cornering.
